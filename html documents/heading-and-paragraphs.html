Headings and Paragraps

Heading One

Headings in html are listed h1 to h6 in order of priority with h1 being the most important while h6 being the least important.

It is important that each webpage has only one h1 to depicate the most important heading usually a title, h2 can be a subtitle while h3 onwards can be subsections

Paragraphs

Html can allow both lowercase and uppercase element tags but it is best practice to use lowercase letters in tags.

The Poem Problem

In html, when we write a poem, the browser will interpret it as a single paragrap as it ignores all the space inbetween.

The 'pre tag' sloves the poem problem.

The War That Followed Home

They once stood smiling
a soldier and his bride,
eyes full of tomorrow,
hearts unscarred by war.

But war took him whole
and returned him in fragments
thin, haunted, breathing but not living.
“Don’t come in,” he whispered,
“I still ain’t out of there.”

The battle had followed him home
in silence, in shadows,
in wounds no eye could see.
She tried to love him whole,
but some battles never end.

Now he walks through each day
like a ghost of peace long lost,
and she stands beside him,
loving what remains.

Some wars end in gunfire
others live forever inside.
